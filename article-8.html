Key Sugar For Life On Earth Could Have Formed In Space

Click on the image below for the original article.
A sugar found in all cells might have formed on ice grains irradiated by ultraviolet light in stellar nurseries.

Summary

Ribose, a molecule that helps carry out the instructions encoded in genes and the backbone of RNA, is found in all life on Earth. In a new study, scientists found out that RNA might have formed in space and later rained down on Earth a long, long time ago. Scientists have found that this sugar can can form when a blend of ices are exposed to UV radiation. Cornelia Meinert, a chemist at University Nice Sophia Antipolis in France, was the one who conducted the study. She and her colleagues started with water, ammonia and methanol, which are found as ice on comets, and cooled the substances to –195 degrees Celsius. Then they simulated the radiation given off by a young star by exposing the ice to UV light. After that, it was warmed back to room temperature, and 55 different organic compounds were found. Ribose was one of those compounds.

Reflection

The title of this article really interested me as I was looking through the ScienceNews website for articles. I took AP Biology, so I knew this was going to be something about ribose. In class, we learned about Earth's evolutionary history and what UV radiation can do, so I saw the connection between this article and what we learned in class. This sounded really interesting, so I clicked on it, and I learned something very fascinating. Apparently many different organic compounds can be made just by exposing water, ammonia, and methanol to UV radiation after cooling it to simulate ice on comets! Because one of the organic substances created was ribose, a molecule that plays a very important role in the genetic makeup of living things, this shows that life on Earth could have started when comets were exposed to the sun and ice from the comets somehow found its way to Earth! This was a fascinating find, because this adds another theory to one of the mysteries of the universe: how life first started. Of course, we can't ever really prove that one of the theories about how life first started is true, but we certainly can conduct tests like the one shown in this article to have a better understanding of the meaning of life.
